ConceptBed

Evaluating Concept Learning Abilities of Text-to-Image Diffusion Models

Maitreya Patel, Tejas Gokhale, Chitta Baral, Yezhou Yang
Arizona State University

Paper (Under Review) Code (soon!) Data Results Explorer

Abstract

The ability to understand visual concepts and being able to replicate, and compose these concepts from images is a central goal for computer vision. Recent advances in text-to-image (T2I) generative models have resulted in high levels of photorealism and have shown promise in understanding visual concepts. However, the evaluation of T2I models has focused on photorealism and qualitative evaluation of visual concepts. The goal of this work is to quantify the ability of T2I models in learning and synthesizing visual concepts. Towards this goal, we introduce ConceptBed, a large-scale dataset that consists of 284 unique visual concepts, more than 5000 unique concept compositions, and more than 33000 composite text prompts. This dataset allows us to evaluate the ability of T2I models to learn and generate visual concepts. We propose $\Delta \mathcal{U}$, an uncertainty-based evaluation metric that uses the confidence of oracle concept classifiers to quantify the alignment between concepts generated by T2I generators and concepts contained in ground truth images. We evaluate visual concepts that are either objects, attributes, or styles, and also evaluate four dimensions of compositionality: counting, attributes, relations, and actions. Our human study shows that $\Delta \mathcal{U}$ is highly correlated with human understanding of concepts thereby demonstrating the effectiveness of our approach. Our results show that there exists a trade-off between learning the concepts and preserving the compositionality and none of the prior works perform satisfactorily.
